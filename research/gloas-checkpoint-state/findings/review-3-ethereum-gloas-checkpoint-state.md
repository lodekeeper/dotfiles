Your skipped-boundary case is real under the current state transition. Gloas applies `execution_requests` only in `process_execution_payload`, and `process_epoch` later consumes pending queues such as `process_pending_deposits` and `process_pending_consolidations`. On your question about changing attestation timing so ordinary attesters know payload status before voting: technically that is possible as a redesign, but it is **not** how current Gloas is structured. The validator flow has ordinary attesters wait for the beacon block and attest by `ATTESTATION_DUE_BPS_GLOAS`, while PTC members wait later for payload/blobs until `PAYLOAD_ATTESTATION_DUE_BPS`. In fork choice, same-slot attestations must have `index == 0`, and `index == 1` requires the payload to already be known locally. So the current protocol intentionally keeps ordinary FFG voting on the pre-payload critical path and pushes payload knowledge into the later PTC/full-attestation path.
